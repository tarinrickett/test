Mykonos

15 Apr. 2017

Mykonos is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the world, and that's evident from the minute you get off the boat. Almost every sign is written in English rather than Greek and waiters were taken aback anytime we threw in "Ya sas" instead of hello or "Efharistó" for thank you. Even though we were there a few weeks before the start of the season, tourists in cargo shorts and branded t-shirts outnumbered Greeks on nearly every street.

To get a real feel for the Greek islands, we purposefully took the time to explore outside the main port, venturing further into the actual country, mountainsides, and coast lines of the island. I'm so glad we did, because the less touristy side of the island was so much more beautiful to explore.

Athens

09 Apr. 2017

It's no lie when they say you can see the Parthenon from across Athens! Eating a gyro and sipping on some a greek coffee is made that much more special when you can glimpse this masterpiece from your café table.

Athens was truly rich in history, with something to see on every corner. We strolled through the Athenian flea markets, gorged ourselves on traditional Greek food, and basked in the sun. It was a short two days in this complicated city, and I can't wait to someday come back!

Normandie

20 Mars 2017

For our final big class excursion, our program took us Americans to see Normandy and the D-Day beaches. Everyone knows the difficult history of WWII, but hearing these stories retold from the spot where these men stood, fought, and died is an entirely different experience.

It was odd to see so many Americans—now easily recognizable to us in their cargo shirts and neon, branded t-shirts— in one spot. When, on the hour, the national anthem started to play over the fields of the American cemetary, and everyone as far as the eye could see stopped and turned to face the American flags, placing a solomn hand over their hearts, it was impossible not to feel at home. It was an emotional weekend to say the least; full of extreme waves of sorrow, pride, and the deepest gratitude.

Morocco

18 Feb. 2017

I have been trying to get to Morocco for a while now and it more than lived up to the hype. The people were friendly, kind, and welcoming; the cities stunning; and the overall culture just beautiful. Even the most mundane bus rides were breathtaking trips through a rocky and mountanous countryside like I'd never seen before.

We definietly hit a few bumps in the road... for example, we got soaked to the bone in a rare thunderstorm that subsequently cancelled our boat across the Straight of Gibraltar*... but the magical time spent bartering (in french!) for hand-woven blankets, following local guides down winding alleyways to buy spices from their friend, or watching a camel start to eat an american girl's scarf made it well worth it.

* We ended up finding another ship to take us over, although this trip was 2 hours longer than the first and left in complete, rainy darkness. The voyage was so rocky that walking (for those of us who could manage it) felt like you were bouncing on the moon.

Rennes

30 Jan. 2017

Rennes was our first weekend getaway! We had a ton of fun exploring this city. We ended up walking 20 miles in 2 days so I'm pretty sure we made our way down almost every street; including their famous Rue de la Soif ("Thisty Street"), named for its number of bars popular with the large student population.

Sunday was drizzly and a great day to get a little more cultured. We toured the local art museum and stopped to watch a show at the planetarium as well. All in all, Rennes was a short and simple local trip. The perfect choice for our first independent adventure.
